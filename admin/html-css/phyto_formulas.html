Raw organism counts were normalized to the sample volume using the following formula:

\(organisms/mL = {CA_{c} \over VA_{f}F}\)

where \(C\) is the organism count, \(A_{c}\) is the area of the cell bottom (mm\(^2\)), \(A_{f}\) is the area of each grid field (mm\(^2\)), \(F\) is the number of fields examined, and \(V\) is the settled volume (mL). This simplifies to:
\(organisms/mL = {C \over cV}\)

where \(cV = {A_{c} \over VA_{f}F}\) and is equal to the counted volume.
